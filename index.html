How μCytoD™ fits into your workflow
Mechanoporation
Stainless-steel deformation zones
Cells are hydrodynamically focused into narrow pores of a Dutch-weave stainless mesh. Repeated, gentle deformations open transient pores that allow cargo entry without permanent damage.
16–20 µm apertures
5 deformation cycles
Viscoelasticity
Low-dose PEO for focusing & protection
A low concentration of high-MW PEO (e.g. 0.5–1 mg/mL) amplifies extensional stresses while cushioning cells, improving delivery even at moderate flow rates.
PEO 8 MDa
0.5–1 mg/mL
Cargo-agnostic
From 4 kDa dextran to mRNA & RNPs
Demonstrated with FITC-dextran (4–2000 kDa) and nuclear eGFP mRNA; compatible with a wide set of nucleic acids, proteins and soft nanoparticles within similar size ranges.
>90% delivery
Cargo size-aware
Step 1
Prepare cells & viscoelastic buffer
Harvest cells, wash, and resuspend in recommended PEO-based buffer at the target cell density. Mix with your cargo (mRNA, RNP, etc.) according to our protocol guide.
BSC-friendly
Standard media
Step 2
Run through μCytoD™ cartridge
Connect cartridge to syringe or pressure pump, set flow rate (e.g. 2 mL/min), and process your sample. Our data explorer shows recommended starting windows by cell type.
Single pass
Up to 10⁷ cells/min
Step 3
Recover, culture, and read out
Collect effluent, dilute into culture media, and incubate. Assess expression or editing using flow cytometry, imaging, or functional assays.

Versus electroporation
Less heating, better viability
Unlike electroporation, μCytoD™ avoids electrical arcs and Joule heating. For many lines, this translates to similar or higher delivery efficiency with improved viability and recovery.

Reduced cell stress
